Golden Ratio - Finance

The Golden Ratio is a natural phenomenon of proportion and symmetry resulting from a pattern of numbers called the Fibonacci sequence. You can apply the Golden Ratio, also referred to as Phi, to anything from the human face to a snowflake. In the stock market, analysts use the Golden Ratio to examine major price movement trends for the market as a whole or for a particular stock. When applying the Golden Ratio to stock market movements, analysts use the smallest positive value of .618, expressed as a percentage or 61.8 percent. Analysts then divide one number in the sequence by the number that is two spaces to the right. For example, 21/55=38.18 rounded up to 38.2 percent. Analysts then divide one number in the sequence by the number that is three spaces to the right. For example, 21/ 89 = 23.6.
